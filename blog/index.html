Cactool

Authors: Liam McLoughlin, Sam Ezeh

Abstract

Cactool is a free open-source platform for researchers undertaking manual content analysis of social media posts. It uses a web-app interface to allow for quick coding of social media posts in their native format. This approach brings new possibilities for coders to work more collaboratively as teams, on the go, and produce valuable research data when compared to the approach of coding tweets and other social media artifacts with spreadsheets. This presentation highlights several key features of the platform, quality-of-life improvements for researchers, Cactool’s application to future research design on memes, and the types of data accepted by the platform (Twitter, Instagram, TikTok, & YouTube).

Keywords: Methods; research software; social media; content analysis; discourse analysis
